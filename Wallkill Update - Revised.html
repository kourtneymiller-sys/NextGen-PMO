Moratorium Navigation & Project Roadmap

We Have a Clear Path — And We're Already in Motion

Three strategic pillars de-risk the moratorium. The April 2026 exemption is the critical gate.

✱ WALMART
Pillar 01
Administrative Exemption Route
Pursuing a direct exemption from the Town — not a public vote. This bypasses the volatility of a formal referendum and keeps the decision in the hands of aligned officials rather than the open ballot.
Pillar 02
100% Union Labor as Political Shield
Committing to full union construction labor gives the project political legitimacy with the Governor — the key ally needed to secure both the moratorium exemption and $65M–$200M in discretionary incentives.
Pillar 03
Legacy Project Framing (2003 Continuity)
Positioning Wallkill as a continuation of the 2003 approved project reduces environmental review triggers and infrastructure demands — lowering the bar for approval and accelerating the exemption pathway.

Critical Path Evaluation — Wallkill, NY vs. Douglas, MA

Douglas Does Not Move the Needle — Wallkill Remains the Right Choice

A ~6-week schedule difference is not a reason to abandon superior network fit and overall project value.

✱ WALMART
The Statutory Black Box: Both sites face a mandatory, non-compressible 12-month environmental review cycle. No amount of capital injection or additional labor can accelerate it — it is a fixed regulatory constraint that anchors both timelines equally.
FY30 is not achievable at either site. Earliest go-live at either location is April–May 2030. The question is site selection — not timeline acceleration.
